Argentina:
your new office

Argentina is the best destination in the world to live and work. If you are a digital nomad, and you are looking for your next adventure, Argentina offers benefits tailored to your needs so that you can have amazing experiences.

Why Argentina?

Convenient

Currently, Argentina is one of the most convenient countries for digital nomads. The relation between the cost of living and the quality of the experiences it offers has made it one of the destinations with the highest growth in 2022 as an option to live and work.

Connected

In the large urban centres of Argentina, such as Buenos Aires, Mendoza, Bariloche, Córdoba, or Salta, the internet connection is good and stable, which is one of the conditions that digital nomads value the most. The possibility of working with an enchanting landscape in the background is at your fingerprints.

Fun

Argentina is a vibrant country for its nightlife, endless culture and liveliness, high-quality wines at unbeatable prices, stunning nature, and wide-ranging gastronomy. What's more, there's an added value that every tourist who has ever visited Argentina mentions: people's warmth and friendliness.

Natural

From the most breath-taking waterfalls in America to the majestic Perito Moreno Glacier… From the jungles of the north to Antarctica… From the delta and estuaries of the east to the Andean deserts of the north… From a megalopolis like Buenos Aires to small hidden towns in Patagonia.

Recommended Experiences

Enjoying Buenos Aires nightlife: Theatre and pizza on Corrientes Street.

Buenos Aires is known as the city that never sleeps, and its nightlife results memorable for anyone who experiences it: it is fun, with a very wide and diverse offer and, most importantly, very safe.

Tasting the best wines in the wineries in Mendoza.

The Andes Mountain surroundings, quaint cities, rural houses, urban accommodation, five-star hotels, international restaurants, signature kitchen… And in that dreamy setting, the opportunity to enjoy the wine experience.

Having a Patagonian beer in Bariloche.

Bariloche beer is considered the best in Argentina due to the quality of the meltwater used in its production. Many Patagonian breweries have been awarded and recognized across the world for their excellent quality and taste.

Going trekking in Chaltén during the summer.

Take up the Fitz Roy challenge. Experience days discovering amazing places, and nights enjoying a canopy of stars. Follow the mystique of expeditionary adventurers! Your eyes will not believe what nature gives us.

Visiting the Iguazú Falls.

The roar of the water, the song of the birds, and the rainforest beauty will hypnotise you in this unique environment. One of the new Seven Natural Wonders of the World, declared a Natural World Heritage Site, which you can't miss.

Touring around Quebrada de Humahuaca with a backpack.

The Quebrada de Humahuaca, declared a Cultural Heritage Site by UNESCO in 2003, is located in the northwest of the country, in the province of Jujuy. Its charm? The high multicoloured mountain ranges tracing the routes that have welcomed at their feet peoples who have contributed culture, tradition, and history to our nation.

Spending a day in a countryside estate and trying asado.

Do you know what asado means in Argentina? Just the best excuse to gather close friends in a good-food ceremony with steps and rules, and, above all, governed by enjoyment. Estates in Buenos Aires are ideal to have the complete experience of trying asado made by true country people.

FAQ

What is a digital nomad?
Digital nomads are people with a high human capital profile, who work remotely. The growing trend of workers who chose to live as digital nomads has increased after the pandemic upon reconsidering and valuing the lifestyle that integrates work and enjoyment.
Do I need a visa to stay in Argentina?
You can stay in Argentina for 90 calendar days upon your arrival without doing any paperwork. If your three-month stay is finished, and you wish to stay longer, you can apply for a special visa for Digital Nomads on the Ministry of the Interior website.
Do I have to pay taxes in Argentina?
No. Digital nomads working remotely are not subject to paying local taxes as long as they do not do any work for Argentina.
What kind of jobs can I do while staying in Argentina?
Any job you can perform remotely for your country's market or another market, provided that it is not for the Argentinian market and/or for an Argentinian employer. That is to say, you are employed by or perform a job through the technology of communication for, a company, or your own company, not registered in Argentina and not performing any work for or providing services to employers (legal or natural persons) in Argentina. If you want to work locally, you have to apply for a work and residence permit in the country.
What language is spoken in Argentina?
The official language of Argentina is Spanish, which is spoken throughout the national territory. English is also spoken very fluently in the major cities.
Where can I find information and recommendations about things to do in Argentina?
On the official website of Visit Argentina.
